Digital Trailblazer Since 2007

I'm part of the GenX generation that grew up alongside the internet. We didn't just witness the digital revolution—we built it. At 58, I recognize that my age and experience are my greatest assets in this field:

  • 2007-2010: Founded and ran Women Internet Marketers, bringing together women from the USA, UK, and other countries interested in internet marketing, complete with a podcast before podcasting was mainstream.
  • 2009: Created Dominican Diaspora, a membership site that grew to over 8,000 members and was eventually sold—proving the power of community building in the early social web days.
  • 2011-2015: Launched Influencers.biz—a WordPress multisite platform long before "influencer" became a buzzword—helping local businesses enhance their online presence through collective blogging and SEO.
  • 2011-2017: Operated Little Fish Digital Media, assisting local business owners with SEO and client acquisition strategies.
  • 2011-2012: Served as membership manager for Blog Success, providing support and guidance to members navigating the then-emerging blogosphere.

I've been invited to speak at the University of the West Indies, teaching authors how to leverage the internet to promote their work—sharing knowledge when digital marketing was still in its infancy.
